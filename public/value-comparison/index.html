How to Read the Cost Per Click Column
Cost Per Click is what an advertiser pays Google each time someone clicks their ad for that keyword. It measures what the organic traffic is worth in ad dollars avoided. Example: a keyword with a $15 Cost Per Click and 460 monthly organic visitors = $6,900/month in ad spend avoided — money the new site earns by ranking, not paying.
